Optimizing Kedah-Kelantan (KK) Cattle Growth Rates

Leveraging calculus to pinpoint the exact moment of maximum growth acceleration in indigenous Malaysian beef cattle to optimize feeding strategies.

The Bioscience Scenario

In Malaysian beef production, managing **Kedah-Kelantan (KK) cattle** efficiently requires understanding their growth velocity. While tracking absolute weight $f(x)$ is important, animal scientists rely on the **derivative $f'(x)$**—representing the Average Daily Gain (ADG)—to determine when the animal is growing most efficiently and when growth begins to plateau, signalling the optimal market or dietary adjustment window.

Data Reference: Growth models adapted from parameters found in the Malaysian Journal of Animal Science (MJAS) and MARDI research papers on KK Cattle growth curves.

Mathematical Modeling

Growth Function (Weight)
f(x) = -0.0004x³ + 0.036x² + 0.15x + 25
Where x = age in weeks, f(x) = weight in kg (Birth weight ≈ 25kg).
Derivative Function (Growth Rate / ADG)
f'(x) = -0.0012x² + 0.072x + 0.15
Where f'(x) = rate of weight gain (kg / week).
